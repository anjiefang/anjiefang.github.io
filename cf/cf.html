Choosing A Community For A Twitter User

Instructions

Overview

In this task, you will be given a Twitter user's profile description along with 10 tweets. By reading the description and 10 tweets, you are asked to choose a category for this Twitter users. These categories are “Academics”, “Media”, “Business”, “Politics” and“Citizen”. Explanations of the 5 categories is shown below:

-Academics: People who are doing research are academic people. They may focus on a specific field, e.g. Chemistry, Mathematics or Social science.These people mostly come-from universities or research institutes. A Twitter user who describes himself/herself as a researcher or who has an academic title (e.g.Professor, Associate Professor, Lecturer, etc.)are academic people. Their tweets may contain the latest techniques, research findings or projects.

-Media: People who work in newspaper/broadcast companies are media people.They can be journalists, reporters, correspondents, etc. Most of them hold a neutral position when reporting some events/news. On Twitter,there is a type of user, who spreads/forwards the latest news or writes news stories by themselves. They may not work for any newspaper/broadcast companies, but they are also media people.

-Business: People from commercial company/industry/manufacture should be labelled as business. They usually post tweets about the new products of their companies. Or, they interact with the other business in similar field on Twitter. Their Twitter description may contain their work title (e.g. Manager, CEO).

-Politics: Politicians are typical politics users. Politicians can be legislators (congress man or MPs), people in a political parties or administration staff in government. Besides, people who are interested in politics should be labelled as politics. Even people don’t have any political affiliations; they should be labelled as“Politics” if they post a lot of politics relevant tweets.

-Citizens: People who apparently don’t belong to “Academics”,“Media” “Business” and “Politics”, should be labelled as  “Citizens”. Most of their tweets are about life. These people may include normal citizens, workers and even famous people, as long as they post a lot life related tweets.

Steps & Rules

  1. Read the given description of a Twitter user. The description may contain a job title, organization name or occupation’s name. You can use this information to label this user to 4 categories.

  2. Read the given 10 tweets. Check whether these tweets are relevant to the 4 categories.

  3. Finally,make a decision and label the given user to one of the 4 categories.

Note that both the user’s description and tweets should imply the same category affiliation. If a user’s description suggests him/her as a politics-interested person, you can only label him/her into politic category when this person has at least two tweets about politic.
